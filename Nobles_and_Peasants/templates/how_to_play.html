What is the Main Page

It has six brown boxes where you can choose a name, pledge allegiance, buy a drink, ban a peasant, get a quest, or kill a target.

What is the Enter Party box?

Every person at the party must have a name. You choose your name and whether you want to be a peasant or a noble.

What is the Pledge Allegiance box?

If you are a peasant, you must pledge allegiance to some noble in order to buy a drink. When you do that, the cost of your drinks will be paid for with that noble's coin.

What is the Buy Drink box?

You use this box to purchase drinks.

What is the Ban Peasant box?

If a peasant is drinking too much and wasting too much of a noble's coin, a noble can ban a peasant from his or her kingdom. That peasant must pledge allegiance to a different noble in order to drink.

What is the Get a Quest box?

Peasants and Nobles receive coin when they perform quests. Peasants want more coin because they need coin to become a noble. Nobles want more coin because, if they run out of coin, they become a peasant. Quests can be performed on behalf of someone else.

What is the Kill a Target box?

When you kill someone, you take their money, their status, and their soldiers.

What is the Game Setup Page?

It has three tables and three brown boxes where you can control the information in those tables.

What is the Add or Update Drink table?

This table shows all of the drinks available to buy at your party. At the start, each party is given water and beer. You can add a drink or update the price of an existing drink. You can have as many drinks as your want.

What is the Update Starting Coin table?

This table shows how much coin Nobles and Peasants receive when they start the game. You can update this amount, so as to match the price of your drinks or how much you and your friends are drinking. You don't want nobles to have too much or too little coin.

What is the Set Quest Rewards table?

This table shows how much coin you will receive for completing different types of quests. You can update this amount, so as to help control how quickly a peasant can become a noble.
